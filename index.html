Marie Curie

The first person to win two Nobel Prizes

Timeline

  • 1867

    Marie Curie, née Maria Salomea Skłodowska, was born in Warsaw on November 7, 1867.

  • 1878

    Marie's mother died of tuberculosis in May, when Marie was ten years old.

  • 1891

    In 1891, Marie went to Paris and enroled at the University of Paris.

  • 1893

    She was awarded a degree in physics and began work in an industrial laboratory of Professor Gabriel Lippmann.

  • 1894

    Marie met Pierre Curie, Professor in the School of Physics, and in the following year they were married.

  • 1897

    In 1897, her daughter Irène was born.

  • 1898

    Marie and her husband published a joint paper announcing the existence of an element which they named "polonium", in honour of her native Poland.

  • 1900

    She became the first woman faculty member at the École Normale Supérieure, and her husband joined the faculty of the University of Paris.

  • 1902

    In 1902 she visited Poland on the occasion of her father's death.

  • 1903

    In June 1903, supervised by Gabriel Lippmann, Marie was awarded her doctorate from the University of Paris. Later in December 1903, the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences awarded Pierre Curie, Marie Curie, and Henri Becquerel the Nobel Prize in Physics.

  • 1904

    Marie gave birth to their second daughter, Ève.

  • 1906

    Following the tragic death of Pierre Curie in 1906, Marie took his place as Professor of General Physics in the Faculty of Sciences, the first time a woman had held this position.

  • 1910

    In 1910 Marie succeeded in isolating radium; she also defined an international standard for radioactive emissions that was eventually named for her and Pierre: the curie.

  • 1911

    She received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in recognition of her work in radioactivity. She was the first person to win or share two Nobel Prizes, and remains alone with Linus Pauling as Nobel laureates in two fields each.

  • 1914

    At the start of World War I, she became the director of the Red Cross Radiology Service and set up France's first military radiology centre. It is estimated that over a million wounded soldiers were treated with her X-ray units.

  • 1922

    Marie became a fellow of the French Academy of Medicine.

  • 1923

    In 1923, she wrote a biography of her husband Pierre, entitled Pierre Curie.

  • 1930

    Marie was elected a member of the International Atomic Weights Committee where she served until her death.

  • 1934

    On 4 July 1934, she died from aplastic anemia believed to have been contracted from her long-term exposure to radiation.
